## Design Context

### Users
Young people aged 16-25 in Tiel (Gelderland) and the surrounding Betuwe region looking to get their first driving license. Primary categories: car (B), motorcycle (A), moped (AM), trailer (BE), and taxi certification. Parents are often the paying party but students find the school via social media and WhatsApp. Mobile-first audience (70%+ traffic). Communication preference: WhatsApp and DM over phone calls or forms.

The job to be done: find a driving school that will actually get me my license — with a real instructor who knows what they're doing, not just the cheapest option.

### Brand Personality
**Energetic, personal, celebratory**

Voice: The proud instructor who posts your celebration photo at CBR the moment you pass. Uses Gen-Z language naturally ("in the pocket", "let's gooo", "slay"). Informal jij/je address. Short punchy sentences mixed with genuine enthusiasm. Never corporate, never template-sounding.

Emotional goals: Excitement to start driving + trust that this will work out. The visitor should feel "this is MY instructor" within 3 seconds.

The customer is NOT: older adults looking for refresher courses, or people shopping purely on price. Budget-seekers and corporate clients should self-select out.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** E7 D6 F3 R6 A5 (energetic, balanced density, very casual, moderate richness, moderate animation)

**Color:** Warm vivid orange (#F05A1E) as anchor on warm off-white (#FFFAF7). Warm-toned neutrals with brown undertones (#211A18 text, #655D5A muted). Dark sections use #1A1A2E. NO blue (market-saturated — all 10 competitors use blue). CTA buttons: dark text on orange, white text on hover-darkened orange (#B62500).

**Typography:** Outfit (geometric sans, 800 weight for H1, 700 for H2-3) + Figtree (body, 400-600). Perfect Fourth scale (1.333). Bold, modern, youthful. NO serif fonts.

**Layout:** Full-bleed hero with dark overlay. SVG wave section dividers (inspired by Rijschool GLOW). Alternating section backgrounds (#fff / #F9EFEB / #FFF0E8). Non-uniform section padding. Card grid with color-shift hover pattern.

**Motion:** Slow-reveal with energetic accents. Fade-up scroll reveals (300ms). Snappy easing: cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1). Signature interaction: CBR stat counters that animate from 0 to final value on scroll.

**Icons:** Phosphor (rounded, friendly — matches F3 casual formality). Stroke width 2px.

**References (approved, HTML saved in competitor-reference/):**
- Nationale Rijschool (9/10) — conversion-optimized, scale-based trust signals, postcode planner
- Rijschool de Betuwe (8/10) — multi-hero sections, instructor profiles, SVG dividers, pill buttons
- Rijschool GLOW (7.6/10) — SVG wave dividers, gradient CTAs, Google badge in hero, photo gallery grid

**Anti-references (explicitly rejected):**
- Xplore Rijopleidingen (4/10) — "old design clearly": dated template, no hierarchy, buried CTAs
- Rijschool Noord (4/10) — "old and broken": Revolution Slider, "Nu kopen" language, stock photos
- Jaddi (5/10) — "slow and old": slow loading, stock imagery, hidden pricing
- Van Thiel (7/10) — "too impersonal": corporate tone, no personality visible

### Design Principles

1. **CBR stats are the hero** — No competitor displays actual CBR exam pass rates. Motor 91%, Bromfiets 90%, Taxi 100% are the primary differentiator. Display them prominently with animated counters. This is what makes the site unique.

2. **Real photos only** — The Facebook celebration photos of real students at CBR are the brand's strongest visual asset. They replace stock photography entirely. Every competitor using stock photos scored lower in client ratings.

3. **WhatsApp first** — The target audience (16-25) communicates via WhatsApp, not forms or phone calls. WhatsApp button should be primary CTA alongside the proefles form. Sticky mobile bar: WhatsApp + "Plan je proefles".

4. **Speed is the competitive edge** — Every competitor runs WordPress (3-5s loads). An Astro static site with ~15KB JS budget delivers sub-second LCP. This performance gap is invisible to the visitor but measurable in conversion rates (+21.6% per 0.1s improvement for lead gen).

5. **The instructor IS the brand** — This is an eenmanszaak (sole proprietor). The site should feel like talking to the instructor, not browsing a corporate brochure. The Gen-Z voice from the Facebook posts should come through in every section.
